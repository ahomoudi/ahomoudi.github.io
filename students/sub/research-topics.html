Identification of Tropical cyclones in the Arabian Sea Sub-Basin

Supervisors: A. Homoudi1 & K. Barfus2 (Chair of Meteorology)

1 ahmed.homoudi@tu-dresden.de

2 klemens.barfus@tu-dresden.de

Background:

Tropical cyclones (Tcs) are one of the most devastating events and are expected to intensify under global warming. TCs are one of the most difficult events to predict and understand in the atmosphere. Storm surges associated with severe TCs are the most damaging rather than rain and strong winds. Under a warming climate, the frequency of the TCs in the Arabian Sea is increasing. Additionally, the TCs are projected to intensify, with a greater proportion of storms having more damaging wind speeds, higher storm surges, and more extreme rainfall rates. Besides the fact that TCs are spatially shifted in Climate models, the detection criteria vary tremendously between climate models and remote sensing data. Therefore, the characteristics of TCs should be understood through direct, objective and automatic detection and tracking methods in reanalyses and model simulations to contribute to developing integrated planning for Climate Change.

Objectives:

  • Detecting tropical cyclones genesis from Remote sensing data (e.g., IR images)
  • Identification of tropical cyclones in ERA5 reanalysis data.
  • Comparison of the findings with observation data (i.e. IBTrACS)
  • Systematic presentation of findings

Skills (not compulsory but preferred or strong motivation to learn):

  • Programming with R or Python
  • Familiarity with Linux and shell scripting
  • Acquaintance with climate data formats (e.g., NetCDF)

Climatology of the Moisture Sources in the Arabian Peninsula using Lagrangian Method

Supervisors: A. Homoudi1 & K. Barfus2 (Chair of Meteorology)

1 ahmed.homoudi@tu-dresden.de

2 klemens.barfus@tu-dresden.de

Background:

The Arabian Peninsula is one of the aridest regions on earth, and it is a region of reciprocal influences of tropical and extra-tropical configurations. Several configurations govern the climate of the region (e.g., Mediterranean, monsoon, subtropical, tropical, and Saharan influences). It is important to understand the configuration contributing to precipitation events in such an arid region. One essential ingredient for precipitation events is the available moisture, which can, e.g. be transported in organised bands known as atmospheric rivers. These different means of moisture transport are responsible for some of the global hydro-climatic extremes. In the Arabian Peninsula, they are responsible for the record floods during March 2019. On the other hand, these extreme precipitation events might be the only source of groundwater recharge.

Objectives:

  • Literature review: Sources of moisture, precipitation systems, Lagrangian analysis.
  • Calculating forward/backward trajectories to infer water vapour transport to the Arabian Peninsula.
  • Identifying trajectories and moisture contributions leading to precipitation events on the Arabian Peninsula.
  • Obtaining water vapour sources and pathways by Lagranto model using ERA5 reanalysis.
  • Systematic presentation of findings with comprehensive interpretation of the results.
